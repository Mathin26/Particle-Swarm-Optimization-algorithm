HOW PSO WORKS

What is Particle Swarm Optimisation?

PSO is a population-based metaheuristic algorithm inspired by the social behaviour of birds flocking or fish schooling. Each "particle" is a candidate solution flying through the search space, guided by its own experience and the swarm's collective knowledge.

The Core Equations

Each particle has a position x and velocity v. At every iteration:

v(t+1) = ω·v(t) + c₁·r₁·(pBest - x(t)) ← cognitive + c₂·r₂·(gBest - x(t)) ← social x(t+1) = x(t) + v(t+1)

Where r₁, r₂ are random numbers in [0,1] drawn each step.

Parameters Explained

  • ω (Inertia) — Controls momentum. High ω = exploration, low ω = exploitation.
  • c₁ (Cognitive) — How strongly a particle is pulled toward its own best position.
  • c₂ (Social) — How strongly a particle is pulled toward the swarm's best position.
  • v_max — Clamps velocity to prevent particles from flying out of bounds.

Topologies

  • Global Best — All particles share one global best. Fast convergence, risk of premature convergence.
  • Ring (Local) — Each particle only sees its two neighbours. Slower but explores more.
  • Random K=3 — Each particle connects to 3 random others. Balanced exploration.

Objective Functions in this Demo

  • Rastrigin — Many local minima. Classic hard benchmark. Global min = 0.
  • Rosenbrock — Banana-shaped valley. Global min = 0 at (1,1).
  • Ackley — Many local minima, flat outer regions. Min = 0 at (0,0).
  • Sphere — Simple parabola. Min = 0 at (0,0). Tests basic convergence.
  • Himmelblau — Four equal global minima. Interesting multi-modal structure.
  • Eggholder — Highly complex, many local minima arranged in an egg pattern.

How to Use This Simulator

  • Press START to run the swarm. Particles appear as cyan dots on the heatmap.
  • The orange star marks the current global best found.
  • Dim yellow dots are each particle's personal best.
  • Adjust parameters mid-run to see their real-time effect.
  • Watch the Convergence Chart to see fitness improving over iterations.
  • Try Ring topology on Rastrigin vs Global to see the exploration difference.
